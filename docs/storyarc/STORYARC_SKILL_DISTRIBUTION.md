# StoryArc Skill Distribution

Skills follow the Codex learning architecture's six top-level keys: listening, speaking, reading, writing, grammar, vocabulary. Every item declares one **primary** skill and up to two **supporting** skills (manifest carries per-item values). Grammar and vocabulary are never taught bare: grammar items are grammar-in-context with a production task; vocabulary intent rides on every item (see vocabulary strategy).

## Design commitments (answering the challenge gate)

1. **Listening and speaking are not underrepresented:** ≥6 of 15 School Core items per grade are speaking- or listening-primary; every Story episode contains ≥1 listening and ≥1 production moment; every grade's Exam Lab has a dedicated speaking assessment and a listening-heavy set.
2. **School Core is not grammar-heavy:** grammar-primary items are capped at 2 per grade (13% of School Core), always contextualized.
3. Writing grows across grades (register ladder: chat → formal email → CV/report), matching the maturity themes.

Reading's low School Core share is deliberate: reading is heavily exercised in Exam Lab (every reading set) and Story Mode functional-reading beats (posters, emails, ads, letters), keeping School Core seats for production skills that only StoryArc can practice interactively.

## Story Mode skill exposure (27 episodes)

Every episode: listening (all NPC dialogue is voiced with captions — asset gap G4) + speaking/production choices. Functional reading beats: sm-g10-04, 07; sm-g11-02; sm-g12-01, 02, 07. Writing beats: sm-g10-07; sm-g11-02; sm-g12-02, 06. Story Mode produces exposure/practice evidence only, except rubric-declared RECALL EVIDENCE beats listed in the journey matrix.

Every skill receives assessed evidence in every grade. Speaking evidence: G10 interview basics → G11 interview + presentation → G12 summative-track interview. Speaking scoring is transcript-based (relevance, grammar, vocabulary, naturalness, clarity of expressed idea); no pronunciation scoring is designed (gap G3); assessed-speaking summative status pends the open decision.

## Difficulty banding (internal, no CEFR claim)

| Band | Grade | Characteristics |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Band 1 | G10 | High-frequency vocabulary; single-clause dominant; concrete familiar topics; short turns |
| Band 2 | G11 | Multi-clause argument; abstract-lite topics (plans, causes, opinions); extended listening |
| Band 3 | G12 | Professional register; abstract futures/conditionals; authentic-style workplace texts |

Bands are content metadata for authoring and validation; they are not learner-facing labels and imply no external framework equivalence (CURRICULUM SOURCE GAP C4).
