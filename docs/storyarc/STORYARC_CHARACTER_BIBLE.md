# StoryArc Character Bible

Approved concept characters: HANA, RYO, JOHN. The authenticated student is the player (no fixed canonical name; runtime display-name placeholder). All characters are original Nexus IP; none may be written as disguised copies of known anime characters. Every recurring character must be documented here before use.

## HANA — Hana Prameswari (`char-hana`)

| Field | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Age range | 15–16 in Grade 10, aging with the cohort to 17–18 in Grade 12 |
| Role | Player's first friend; co-founder of the revived Bridge Club; club president from Grade 11 |
| Track | SMA (social sciences leaning) |
| Personality | Outgoing, fast-talking, optimistic organizer; genuinely kind; reads people well |
| Strengths | Initiative, networking, event planning, encouraging others to speak |
| Flaws | Overcommits and hides being overwhelmed; vague when avoiding conflict ("it's fine, whatever works"); promises before planning |
| Communication style | Warm, informal, expressive; heavy use of natural conversational chunks |
| English characteristics | The **fluency model**: natural, idiomatic teen-appropriate English with occasional imprecision. Her vagueness is a designed comprehension challenge — players must ask clarifying questions. She never models grammar errors as correct. |
| Relationship to player | First friend at the gate on day one; co-leader; her trust value reflects reliability and honesty in choices |
| Narrative purpose | Drives club goals; her overcommitment creates the Grade 11 conflict arc; her Grade 12 dilemma (scholarship abroad vs. family expectations) carries the "important choices" theme |
| Pedagogical purpose | Source of natural expressions and social English; scenes with Hana train naturalness, small talk, invitations, and softening language |
| Recurring behaviors | Names plans before thinking them through; carries a battered planner; says "okay okay okay" when stressed; celebrates small wins loudly |
| Prohibited inconsistencies | Never cruel or manipulative; never suddenly shy; never speaks broken English; never abandons the club; no romantic storyline with John |

## Cross-character rules

1. Hana, Ryo, and secondary characters speak canonical published dialogue only; John's AI-backed turns run through the approved StoryArc John context.
2. Character English quality is a controlled pedagogical variable: Hana = naturalness model, Ryo = accuracy/directness contrast, John = coaching model, Maya = fluent peer, Ibu Ratna/Pak Halim = formal register. No character models grammatical errors presented as correct.
3. Relationship values exist for Hana, Ryo, and Sari (Grade 11+). They gate optional scenes and dialogue warmth, never learning content access or mastery.
4. No character references copyrighted anime, real celebrities, or real institutions.
