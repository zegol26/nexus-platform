# StoryArc Universe Bible

Original Nexus IP. Anime-inspired describes visual language and pacing only. No copyrighted characters, universes, music, or distinctive compositions. All names below are invented for StoryArc.

## Premise

The player — the authenticated student, addressed by their profile display name via runtime placeholder, never a hardcoded canonical name — arrives at **Kampus Terpadu Cakrawala**, a fictional integrated SMA–SMK campus in the fictional Indonesian coastal city of **Tanjung Laras**. On their first day they are pulled into reviving the school's nearly defunct **Global Communication Club** (students call it the **Bridge Club**), alongside Hana and Ryo, with English mentor John as advisor. Across three grades the club grows from three members into the campus's connection to the outside world — hosting exchange visitors, running the inter-school **Bridge Festival**, and finally carrying each member toward their own future.

English is the story's engine, not its decoration: every arc's central problem is a communication problem — introducing yourself, recruiting strangers, hosting a guest, defusing a conflict, presenting in public, surviving an internship, passing an interview.

### Tanjung Laras

Mid-size coastal city; port, tourism, and a growing creative/tech sector — justifying English relevance for both academic and vocational futures. No real-city landmarks are referenced.

## Tone

Warm, contemporary, grounded slice-of-life with light comedic beats and earned emotional payoffs. Anime-inspired pacing: cold opens, episode stingers, expressive character reactions. No fantasy combat, no supernatural elements, no romance subplots between mentor and students, no dating-sim framing. Player-character relationships are friendship/trust-based.

## The Bridge Club

- Founded years ago, dormant when the story begins; the club room is the story's home base.
- Club rule (in-fiction): inside the club room and at club events, members practice English — a diegetic reason dialogue is in English.
- Club goals per grade: Grade 10 — survive (recruit 3 signatures, host an exchange visitor); Grade 11 — build (win funding, run the Bridge Festival); Grade 12 — hand over (mentor juniors, secure each member's future).

## Core RPG loop (per approved game architecture)

Exploration of campus locations → social interaction with NPCs → language choices with consequences → quests (club goals) → relationship values (Hana, Ryo, secondary NPCs) → learning recall woven into later scenes. No combat mechanics.

## Continuity rules

1. The player is always the authenticated student; dialogue uses a display-name placeholder.
2. Time moves forward only; each grade is one season; no reset of established relationships.
3. Objects and promises persist (e.g., the club signboard painted in S1 appears in S3 graduation).
4. Characters remember prior-season events; recall scenes explicitly reference where language was first learned.
5. Indonesian daily life is depicted respectfully and generically; religion, politics, and real brands/institutions are not plot elements.
6. English in-world is a skill characters are building; imperfect English from characters is intentional and pedagogically labeled (see Character Bible), never mocked.
7. No copyrighted anime references by characters, posters, or parody names.

## Naming conventions

Original Indonesian-plausible names; machine IDs are semantic and IP-neutral (`loc-clubroom`, `char-hana`). Season/arc/episode titles are original (see `STORYARC_SEASON_ARC_PLAN.md`).
